Across 359 QRS codelists probed, 28% have a container but no instrument-level NCIt concept to anchor them — questions exist, but the classification tree is incomplete.

Nearly half of the 2,208 instrument concepts (988) exist for classification only, with no mapping to an SDTM codelist.

The gap is not in any single standard: NCIt classifies but does not compose; CT names TESTCDs but does not describe instrument families, subscales, or version lineage.

An instrument composition registry would carry stable identifiers for instruments, their subscales, their item membership, and their version relationships — linking classification to composition with explicit is composed of edges rather than the weak is related to associations NCIt currently offers.
