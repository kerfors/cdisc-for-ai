Chest X-Ray — The COSMoS Story

How one imaging concept participates in two domain classes — as a procedure-as-intervention in PR, and as a qualifier of measurement-as-findings in MK — and what COSMoS DSS records of each

Why this story splits across two domain classes. A chest X-ray has two SDTM footprints. The procedure itself — the act of acquiring the image — is an Interventions-class record in PR. The measurements read off the image (joint erosion score, cardiothoracic ratio, lesion size) are Findings-class records in body-system domains (MK, CV, TR, …). COSMoS publishes DSSs for both sides, with very different internal logic.

On the PR side the DSS pins identity: PRDECOD is assigned to a specific CDISC CT term from the PROCEDUR codelist. On the Findings side the DSS pins the test but leaves the acquisition modality open as a --METHOD value_list, so the same test can be acquired by X-ray or MRI without multiplying DSSs.

What XRAYCHEST pins vs leaves open

Architectural pattern on the PR side

Each PR DSS is a procedure identity pin: PRDECOD is assigned to a single CDISC CT PROCEDUR term, optionally combined with a PRLOC anatomical pin. The DSS discriminates procedures by (modality, anatomical target). Modality lives as a value of PRDECOD, not as a variable in its own right.

This means: if two DSSs differ only in modality (XRAYCHEST vs CTSCANCHEST), they are two separate DSSs with distinct vlm_group_ids and distinct PRDECOD assignments. Modality does not parameterise a single DSS on the PR side.

The measurement side — SGBESCR DSS in MK (Findings)

When a chest X-ray is read for measurements — joint erosion scores in RA, bone density, cardiothoracic ratio — the measurement records are Findings-class rows in body-system domains. MK holds musculoskeletal measurements; TR holds tumour measurements; CV holds cardiac measurements.

Pinned by the DSS: MKTESTCD = SGBESCR, MKTEST = Sharp/Genant Bone Erosion Score. That is the test-identity pin.

Left open as slot, with codelist constraint: MKMETHOD accepts X-RAY or MRI; MKLAT accepts RIGHT or LEFT; MKORRES/MKSTRESC/MKSTRESN accept the ordinal scale values. The DSS defines the permitted set, the record picks one.

Architectural pattern on the Findings side

Each MK Sharp-scoring DSS is a test identity pin: MKTESTCD is assigned, and the acquisition modality is a --METHOD slot with a value_list (X-RAY or MRI). One DSS covers two modalities.

This is the inverse of the PR pattern. On the Findings side, modality parameterises the same DSS; on the PR side, modality IS the DSS identity. The asymmetry is structural: PR models the procedure-as-event where "what procedure was it" is the identity question, while Findings models the measurement-as-data where "what was measured" is identity and "how it was acquired" is a qualifier.

The same dataset reference (Dataset Specialization section of the SDTMIG) does not produce uniform logic across domain classes. --METHOD value_list breadth varies too: the TR/TU RECIST DSSs accept 15 imaging methods in a single DSS (CT SCAN; CALIPER MEASUREMENT METHOD; ENDOSCOPY; LYMPHANGIOGRAPHY; MAMMOGRAPHY; MRI; NUCLEAR RADIOLOGY; PET SCAN; PET/CT SCAN; PET/MRI SCAN; PHOTOGRAPHY; SCINTIGRAPHY; TOTAL BODY RADIOGRAPHY; ULTRASOUND; X-RAY); MK accepts 2 (X-RAY;MRI); some domains have no MKMETHOD slot at all.

Summary — the two domain-class stories about the same concept

PR side: X-ray is a procedure identity. One PROCEDUR codelist term (X-RAY, C38101) pins PRDECOD in multiple DSSs, each anatomically specialised via PRLOC. Modality differences drive DSS proliferation — XRAYCHEST, CTSCANCHEST, MRIBRAIN are separate rows in the DSS package.

MK side: X-ray is a method qualifier. The same DSS (SGBESCR) covers X-ray and MRI via a value_list on MKMETHOD. Test identity is the driver; modality is subordinate.

The PR record and the Findings record both exist in a real data set when a chest X-ray is measured for Sharp/Genant scoring. They are related via timing (same MKDTC ≈ PRSTDTC), by subject (USUBJID), and optionally by RELREC. That link is not modelled in COSMoS — it lives in the SDTM record layer and in sponsor metadata.
