Chest X-Ray — The COSMoS Story

How one imaging concept participates in two domain classes — as a procedure-as-intervention in PR, and as a qualifier of measurement-as-findings in MK / TR / TU — and what COSMoS DSS records of each

Why this story splits across two domain classes. A chest X-ray has two SDTM footprints. The procedure itself — the act of acquiring the image — is an Interventions-class record in PR. The measurements read off the image (joint erosion score, cardiothoracic ratio, lesion size) are Findings-class records in body-system domains (MK, CV, TR, …). COSMoS publishes DSSs for both sides, with very different internal logic.

On the PR side the DSS pins identity: PRDECOD is assigned to a specific CDISC CT term from the PROCEDUR codelist. On the Findings side the DSS pins the test but leaves the acquisition modality open as a --METHOD value_list, so the same test can be acquired by X-ray or MRI without multiplying DSSs.

Architectural pattern on the PR side

Each PR DSS is a procedure identity pin: PRDECOD is assigned to a single CDISC CT PROCEDUR term, optionally combined with a PRLOC anatomical pin. The DSS discriminates procedures by (modality, anatomical target). Modality lives as a value of PRDECOD, not as a variable in its own right.

This means: if two DSSs differ only in modality (XRAYCHEST vs CTSCANCHEST), they are two separate DSSs with distinct vlm_group_ids and distinct PRDECOD assignments. Modality does not parameterise a single DSS on the PR side.

The measurement side — SGBESCR DSS in MK (Findings)

Architectural pattern on the Findings side

Each MK Sharp-scoring DSS is a test identity pin: MKTESTCD is assigned, and the acquisition modality is a --METHOD slot with a value_list (X-RAY or MRI). One DSS covers two modalities.

This is the inverse of the PR pattern. On the Findings side, modality parameterises the same DSS; on the PR side, modality IS the DSS identity. The asymmetry is structural: PR models the procedure-as-event where "what procedure was it" is the identity question, while Findings models the measurement-as-data where "what was measured" is identity and "how it was acquired" is a qualifier.

The same dataset reference (Dataset Specialization section of the SDTMIG) does not produce uniform logic across domain classes. --METHOD value_list breadth varies too: the TR/TU RECIST DSSs accept 15 imaging methods in a single DSS (CT SCAN; CALIPER MEASUREMENT METHOD; ENDOSCOPY; LYMPHANGIOGRAPHY; MAMMOGRAPHY; MRI; NUCLEAR RADIOLOGY; PET SCAN; PET/CT SCAN; PET/MRI SCAN; PHOTOGRAPHY; SCINTIGRAPHY; TOTAL BODY RADIOGRAPHY; ULTRASOUND; X-RAY); MK accepts 2 (X-RAY;MRI); some domains have no MKMETHOD slot at all. Part 2B
The same pattern in oncology — TR and TU (Findings)

MK is one of three Findings domains whose --METHOD value_list accepts X-Ray. The other two are TR (Tumor/Lesion Results, 9 DSSs) and TU (Tumor/Lesion Identification, 6 DSSs). Both adopt the same modality-as-qualifier pattern as MK Sharp scoring, but with substantially different breadth — a 15-method imaging panel rather than 2 — and with BC anchors that are method-agnostic measurement concepts rather than named scoring instruments.

What LDIAM pins vs leaves open

Pinned by the DSS: TRTESTCD = LDIAM, TRTEST = Longest Diameter, plus a unit pin TRORRESU = TRSTRESU = mm. The unit pin is unusual — it's a measurement-spec assertion that this BC always reports millimetres.

Left open as slot, with codelist constraint: TRMETHOD accepts the full 15-method imaging panel; TREVAL discriminates investigator from independent assessor; TREVALID names up to three radiologists. Identifiers (REFID, LNKID, LNKGRP) link tumour records across visits and between TR and TU.

Architectural contrast with MK Sharp. The Sharp BCs are scoring-instrument concepts (NCIt anchor IS the named instrument). LDIAM's BC is a generic geometric measurement (Diameter → Longest Diameter) — modality-agnostic by NCIt design. The same C96684 concept applies whether the longest diameter is read off an X-ray, a CT, an MRI, or callipers. That's why TR's value_list is so wide.

Cross-domain pattern — modality as qualifier across MK / TR / TU

Structural consistency. All three Findings domains use the same shape: BC anchors a measurement-or-identification concept; --METHOD is a qualifier slot bound to the METHOD codelist (C85492); a value_list restricts the modality acceptance set. Test identity (TESTCD) is the driver; modality is subordinate. None of the three uses imaging modality as a BC anchor.

Where they differ. Value_list breadth and BC nature:

— MK Sharp BCs are named scoring instruments (Sharp/Genant, Sharp/Van der Heijde) anchored to specific NCIt concepts. The X-RAY;MRI value_list is narrow because the scoring methodology was developed for these two modalities specifically.

— TR / TU BCs are method-agnostic measurement and identification concepts (Longest Diameter, Longest Perpendicular, Lymph Node State, Tumor Identification, Tumor Merged, Tumor Split). NCIt models these as geometry / state / counting concepts, independent of how the image was acquired. The 15-method imaging panel reflects that — any imaging route that resolves the geometry is permitted.

Cross-class join via NCIt code C38101 (X-Ray Imaging). The shared NCIt anchor reaches:

— PR side as the BC anchor of 2 DSSs: XRAY, XRAYCHEST.

— Findings side as a value_list member of --METHOD in 20 DSSs: 5 MK Sharp + 9 TR + 6 TU.

That gives 2 × 20 = 40 (PR DSS, Findings DSS) pairs reachable from the X-Ray modality alone, without authoring. The join is computable from cosmos-graph/COSMoS_Graph.xlsx Variables sheet against the BC sheet.

Modality compatibility ≠ clinical pairing. The 40 pairs express what the standards permit — that is, "X-Ray can produce a record matching this Findings DSS schema". They do not assert clinical typicality: XRAYCHEST + Sharp Hand/Wrist is a permitted modality pairing, but Sharp scores hand and foot joints, not chest. Anatomical co-occurrence (PRLOC vs MKLOC / TULOC) is a sponsor- and protocol-level decision, not a COSMoS-level fact.

Part 3
Joined-view projection — consumer-bases DSS_View

The consumer-bases/ track joins the COSMoS graph (BC + DSS + Variables + AssignedTerms + Coding) with repo-side reference metadata (SDTM domain class, test-code identity) into a single denormalised row per DSS. The output is consumer-bases/interim/DSS_View.xlsx, sheet Measurement_Specs.

The pivot is deliberately disciplined: it carries pinned Variables values (assigned terms, with their NCIt and bound codelist), not slot value_lists. Recovering the value_list breadth — the X-RAY;MRI on MKMETHOD, the Y on PRPRESP, the 15-method TR/TU panel — requires going back to cosmos-graph/interim/COSMoS_Graph.xlsx sheet Variables.

Summary — the two domain-class stories about the same concept

PR side: X-ray is a procedure identity. One PROCEDUR codelist term (X-RAY, C38101) pins PRDECOD in multiple DSSs, each anatomically specialised via PRLOC. Modality differences drive DSS proliferation — XRAYCHEST, CTSCANCHEST, MRIBRAIN are separate rows in the DSS package.

MK side: X-ray is a method qualifier. The same DSS (SGBESCR) covers X-ray and MRI via a value_list on MKMETHOD. Test identity is the driver; modality is subordinate.

What the graph-fed projection makes queryable. The shared NCIt anchor is in the data: BC bc_id=C38101 on the PR side ≡ NCIt code referenced by the MKMETHOD value_list on the Findings side. The cross-class join — "imaging procedures with BC anchor X reach measurement DSSs that accept X as method" — is computable from cosmos-graph alone, without authoring. It expresses modality compatibility, not clinical pairing: XRAYCHEST shares C38101 with all five MK Sharp-scoring DSSs by modality, but anatomical co-occurrence (PRLOC=CHEST vs MK joints of hand/wrist/foot) is sponsor- and protocol-level, not COSMoS-level.

The PR record and the Findings record both exist in a real data set when a chest X-ray is measured for Sharp/Genant scoring. They are related via timing (same MKDTC ≈ PRSTDTC), by subject (USUBJID), and optionally by RELREC. That link is not modelled in COSMoS — it lives in the SDTM record layer and in sponsor metadata.
