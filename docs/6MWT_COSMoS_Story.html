6-Minute Walk Test (6MWT) — The COSMoS Story

What COSMoS provides for recording and what it had to invent for composition and classification

Why this story spotlights composition/classification. 6MWT is an instrument-based Finding (FT). Its 6 TESTCDs share a uniform recording spec — same data type, same units, same role pattern across all of them. So the per-variable recording detail is shown once (SIXMW101) and summarised for the rest. The COSMoS contribution that carries the weight is the classification/composition layer: the instrument-level and container-level BCs, the hierarchy path, and the editorial category strings (Functional Assessment, QRS, QRS Instrument Questions) that tie the 6 questions into an instrument and a container. The companion Glucose Story shows the opposite asymmetry — for specimen-based Findings the classification is thin and the recording spec is where the variation lives.

Part 1
What COSMoS provides — the recording specification
COSMoS was designed to specify how a result is recorded at the SDTM variable level. Each DSS is not a single row — it is a multi-variable specification that maps a TESTCD to a complete set of SDTM variables with their roles, assigned values, codelists, data types, and origins. Part 2
What COSMoS had to provide beyond recording — composition and classification
To organise recording specs into navigable structure, COSMoS needed to provide what NCIt does not: composition and classification layers. It created BCs at the instrument and container levels — not for recording, but for grouping and discovery. The contribution increases from thin at the top to rich at the TESTCD level, because COSMoS was designed bottom-up from the recording specification.

The dark gold tags (Functional Assessment, QRS, QRS Instrument Questions) are COSMoS-original editorial classifications — they exist nowhere in NCIt or CT. The grey tags are derivable from NCIt synonyms and codelist names. COSMoS had to invent the classification layer because NCIt only provides "is a kind of", not "belongs to" or "is used for".
Registry need
An instrument composition registry
For 6MWT, COSMoS fills the recording specification cleanly — ten SDTM variables per TESTCD, fully specified with roles, codelists, assigned values, and origins. What COSMoS had to invent beyond recording — the classification strings (Functional Assessment, QRS) at the instrument and container levels — is editorial grouping, not governed composition. Subscales, instrument families (FACT-B = FACT-G + Breast Cancer Concern), and version lineage remain unrepresented. An instrument composition registry would own these relationships with stable identifiers, turning what is now sponsor-by-sponsor interpretation into addressable structure.
