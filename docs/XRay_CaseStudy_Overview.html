Chest X-Ray Case Study — Overview

How X-Ray surfaces in CDISC standards, and what the two companion documents cover

Reading note: assumes working familiarity with CDISC SDTM, SDTM Controlled Terminology, and the COSMoS BC/DSS package. Concepts not covered here are explained in the companion documents.

What this case study is

Chest X-Ray is a single clinical concept with two distinct CDISC footprints. The procedure itself — the act of acquiring an image — is recorded as an Interventions-class event in the PR domain. Measurements read off the image (joint erosion scores, cardiothoracic ratio, lesion size) are recorded as Findings-class data in body-system domains (MK, TR, TU, CV, …). COSMoS publishes Dataset Specializations (DSSs) for both sides, with very different internal logic.

The case study uses Chest X-Ray to make three architectural questions concrete: how the same clinical concept is modelled differently across observation classes; what cross-class linkage is computable from COSMoS alone vs. requires authoring; and where the standards stop and sponsor / SoA / protocol-level work begins.

Key analytical findings

PR and Findings model imaging modality inversely COSMoS Story · Parts 1, 2A, 2B
On the PR side, modality IS the DSS identity — XRAYCHEST, CTSCANCHEST, MRIBRAIN are separate DSSs differentiated by their PRDECOD pin. On the Findings side, the same DSS accepts multiple modalities via a --METHOD value_list — modality parameterises a single DSS rather than multiplying it.
Cross-class join is computable from cosmos-graph alone — 40 PR↔Findings DSS pairs reachable from X-Ray COSMoS Story · Part 2B
The shared NCIt code C38101 (X-Ray Imaging) appears as the BC anchor of 2 PR DSSs (XRAY, XRAYCHEST) and as a --METHOD value_list member in 20 Findings DSSs (5 MK Sharp + 9 TR RECIST + 6 TU RECIST). That gives 2 × 20 = 40 (PR DSS, Findings DSS) pairs the X-Ray modality can produce — a single SQL-equivalent operation over cosmos-graph/COSMoS_Graph.xlsx, no editorial authoring required.
BC nature drives value_list breadth on the Findings side COSMoS Story · Part 2B
MK Sharp BCs (Sharp/Genant, Sharp/Van der Heijde) are named scoring instruments; their --METHOD value_list is narrow (X-RAY;MRI) because the scoring methodology was developed for those modalities. TR/TU BCs (Longest Diameter, Tumor Identification) are method-agnostic measurement and identification concepts; their value_list is the full 15-method imaging panel because the geometry or state can be resolved by any of them.
Modality compatibility ≠ clinical pairing COSMoS Story · Part 2B summary
The 40 cross-class pairs express what the standards permit — "X-Ray can produce a record matching this Findings DSS schema". They do not assert clinical typicality: XRAYCHEST + MK Sharp Hand/Wrist is a permitted modality match, but Sharp scores hand and foot joints, not chest. Anatomical co-occurrence is sponsor- and protocol-level, not COSMoS-level.
Patient body position is absent from every imaging DSS PatientBurden Story · Part 2 + Summary
SDTM CT publishes a governed POSITION codelist (C71148) with 17 terms (STANDING, SUPINE, SEMI-RECUMBENT, …). COSMoS curated --POS as a slot in EG (31 rows) and VS (9 rows) DSSs — but in zero rows of any PR, MK, TR, or TU DSS. This holds across all 20 X-Ray-accepting Findings DSSs uniformly. Position has been curated where it was identified as clinically relevant for blood-pressure and ECG; it has not been curated for imaging.
Joined-view projection carries pins, not slot value_lists COSMoS Story · Part 3
The repo's joined-view layer (consumer-bases/interim/DSS_View.xlsx, sheet Measurement_Specs) projects each DSS as one row with per-slot triplets <SLOT>_value / _ncit / _codelist — but only for assigned (pinned) values. Slot value_lists (the X-RAY;MRI on MKMETHOD, the 15-method panel on TRMETHOD/TUMETHOD, the Y on PRPRESP) do NOT survive the projection — recovering them requires reading the COSMoS Variables sheet directly.
Record-to-record link (RELREC) is named, not modelled COSMoS Story · Part 2B summary; PatientBurden Story · throughout
When a chest X-ray is taken and the resulting image is read for Sharp/Genant scoring, the actual data set has a PR record (the procedure) and an MK record (the measurement) related by timing (MKDTC ≈ PRSTDTC), subject (USUBJID), and optionally RELREC. That record-level link lives in the SDTM data layer and in sponsor metadata, NOT in COSMoS.
For the sponsor
What this case study can support, and what it cannot

Supports: Architectural decisions about how to structure SDTM submissions involving X-Ray procedures and downstream Findings; reasoning about which qualifier dimensions the standards do and do not capture; identification of the COSMoS coverage gap on patient body position for imaging; framing of the SoA-to-SDTM bridge for protocol design.

Does not support (out of scope here): Actual study data submission decisions; sponsor-specific data dictionaries; controlled-vocabulary extension proposals beyond what is documented in the four options. The case-specialisation IDs (ID101 / ID102 / ID103) in the PatientBurden Story are syntactic placeholders, not references to any sponsor catalogue.
