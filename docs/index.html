CDISC for AI

Machine-actionable reference files, plus case-study walkthroughs tracing clinical cases through the SDTM CT + NCIt + COSMoS stack.

Machine-actionable reference files built from CDISC SDTM Controlled Terminology, NCIt, and COSMoS Biomedical Concepts and Dataset Specializations. The purpose is to make existing standards more consumable for AI, rule engines, and humans.

Source repository: github.com/kerfors/cdisc-for-ai

Reference files

Built on a layered approach: cosmos-graph projects COSMoS into a multi-sheet traversable graph (BC, DSS, Variables, Codelists, BC_Parents, BC_Categories); consumer-bases joins the graph into per-DSS views; sdtm-findings-graph produces sub-typed Findings consumer outputs.

  • SDTM_Test_Identity.xlsx domain-level TESTCDs with NCIt identity
  • Specimen_Findings.xlsx specimen-based Findings consumer view (LB, MB, MI, CP, BS, MS, PC, PP)
  • Measurement_Findings.xlsx measurement-based Findings consumer view (VS, MK, CV)
  • Instrument_Findings.xlsx instrument-based Findings consumer view (QS, FT, RS); four-sheet design (Test_Identity, Measurement_Specs, BC_Categories, BC_Parents) adding C20993 + C211913 anchors per row, BC categories search-tag sheet, and parent-chain traversal sheet — makes instrument grouping addressable directly

Case-study walkthroughs

Two case pairs, one in Findings / specimen territory and one in COA / instrument territory. Each pair traces a single clinical case through the SDTM CT + NCIt + COSMoS stack and closes with an open question on where a governed cross-sponsor layer could complement today's standards.

Glucose — specimen-test qualification and case-specialization case (Findings; LB)

  • Glucose Measurement — The COSMoS Story What COSMoS captures as recording specifications, and how the cdisc-for-ai cosmos-graph projection and consumer-bases joined views shape it into the Specimen_Findings consumer file
  • Glucose Measurement — The Study-Intent Story What study designers author on top of GLUCPL — and why the recording specification is not the whole case

6MWT — instrument composition case (COA; QS / FT / RS)

  • 6-Minute Walk Test (6MWT) — The NCIt Story What SDTM CT + NCIt provides when it works — and why it only works for specific cases
  • 6-Minute Walk Test (6MWT) — The COSMoS Story What COSMoS provides for recording and what it had to invent for composition and classification

Legacy files (backward compatibility)

Earlier-generation files retained for users who depend on them. Superseded by the graph-fed reference files above; will retire once parity is fully validated downstream.

  • COSMoS_BC_DSS.xlsx flattened COSMoS Biomedical Concepts and Dataset Specializations — single-sheet interim, predecessor to the multi-sheet cosmos-graph projection
  • Specimen_Findings.xlsx (legacy) specimen-based Findings consumer view, pre-graph build (reads cosmos-bc-dss flatten)
  • Measurement_Findings.xlsx (legacy) measurement-based Findings consumer view, pre-graph build
