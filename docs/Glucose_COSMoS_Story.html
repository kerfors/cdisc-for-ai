Glucose Measurement — The COSMoS Story

What COSMoS captures as recording specifications, and how the cdisc-for-ai cosmos-graph projection and consumer-bases joined views shape it into the Specimen_Findings consumer file

Why this story spotlights the recording spec. Glucose Measurement is a specimen-based Finding (LB). Its classification is thin — one BC sitting under Chemistry Test > Laboratory Tests — but it expands into multiple DSSs because clinically meaningful glucose recording IS the combination of TESTCD × specimen × method × scale × LOINC × unit.

The COSMoS contribution that carries the weight is the per-DSS recording spec, and the case-defining attributes that distinguish GLUCPL from GLUCBLD from GLUCSER and so on.

The companion 6MWT Story shows the opposite asymmetry — for instrument-based Findings the recording spec is uniform and the classification/composition layer is where the variation lives.

Part 1
What COSMoS provides — case-distinct recording specifications

NCIt carries two concepts — C105585 Glucose Measurement and C163446 Plasma Equivalent Glucose — and SDTM CT reflects both as distinct TESTCDs: GLUC (C105585) and GLUCPE (C163446). COSMoS has curated them together under a single BC (C105585 Glucose Measurement). GLUCPE's C163446 remains visible at the TESTCD level and is flagged as a legacy cross-reference in the COSMoS QC (TESTCD_NCIt ≠ BC NCIt, QC-14).

Under that one BC, COSMoS expands into eight DSSs because clinically meaningful glucose recording IS the combination of TESTCD × specimen × method × scale × LOINC × unit. Each DSS is a value-level metadata (VLM) group anchored on LB.LBTESTCD — a bundle of rows that jointly constrain the recording when LBTESTCD takes the relevant value under the relevant conditions. The DS_Code is the vlm_group_id that addresses the bundle.

One DSS (GLUCPL) is shown expanded with its full 12-variable spec; the others are summarised by their case-defining attributes. A further DSS (GLUCSERPL) exists in source but is omitted from this view pending QC review.

The cdisc-for-ai cosmos-graph track preserves COSMoS as a multi-sheet graph (BC, DSS, Variables, Codelists, BC_Parents, BC_Categories) — the source projection. The consumer-bases track then joins these sheets into one row per DSS in DSS_View.xlsx — the joined view that consumer tracks read.

A note on names. From Part 1 onward, column labels carry both names: DS_Code / vlm_group_id, DS_Name / short_name, BC_Name / short_name. The first is the cdisc-for-ai consumer-facing name; the second is the COSMoS source field.

The rebrand is for readability — vlm_group_id is implementation-oriented and short_name is reused at both BC and DSS levels in source.

Pairing preserves source traceability; the cosmos-graph LinkML schemas in cosmos-graph/reference/cosmos_linkml/ document the COSMoS source field names, and the consumer-facing names are applied in consumer-bases/ and the sdtm-findings-graph/ consumer notebooks.

Registry need
A specimen-test qualification registry

The recording specification for GLUCPL is complete as delivered — the VLM group binds variables, units, LOINC slots, and default values on the LB row.

What the stack does not carry is a governed, stable identifier for the qualified measurement itself — Glucose × Plasma × Quantitative with its recording attributes.

Today this identity is addressed via DS_Code mnemonics (GLUCPL, GLUCSERPL, …) that are editorial strings, not unique across domains, and not cross-referenceable.

A specimen-test qualification registry would assign each analyte × specimen × method × scale combination a stable ID with governance, version lineage, and cross-references to NCIt and LOINC — turning the DS_Code layer from mnemonic to identifier.
